Grace Hopper

About

Grace Hopper was a computer scientist and a US rear admiral. She was the reason for many improvements related to technology, which led to her success and fame.

Childhood and Early Life

Grace tried to join the Navy during the early stages of World War Two but was denied for a variety of reasons. In 1943 she obtained a leave of absence from Vassar and entered the US Navy Reserve. Grace trained at Smith College and graduated first in her class one year later. After she exited Smith College she was assigned to the Bureau of Ships Computation Project in Harvard where she worked on the Mark I computer with Howard Aiken.

Inventing

ater in 1949, Hopper joined the Eckert-Mauchly Computer Corporation where she was in team developing the UNIVAC I. This was when Grace started believing in the idea of a programming language that used only english words. Three years later the first paper on compilers was made. Her creation of the linker lead to the beginning of COBOL, which is still the biggest business language.
