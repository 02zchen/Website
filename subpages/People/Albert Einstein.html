Albert Einstein

About

Albert Einstein was born in Germany in the year 1879. His parents were both Jewish and Einstein originally was as well. Einstein began to read books on science which in turn contradicted his religious beliefs. Einstein slowly became more and more infatuated with the scientific world. Einstein went on to develop his theories of relativity and won a nobel prize in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.

Childhood and Early Life

Einstein had one sister, Maria, alongside with his father Hermann Einstein and mother Pauline Koch. There were two main components to Einstein’s fascination with science. The first was the compass which he first saw at age 5. This began his fascination with invisible forces. The second was a geometry book he had gotten at age 12. Einstein had a friend, Max Talmud, that also helped to tutor Einstein and introduce him to new mathematical and philosophical books and ideologies. At age 16, Einstein’s father’s company began to fail. Hermann and Pauline moved towards Zurich and left Einstein in a boarding house in Munich to finish his education. With pressure growing on Einstein to join the military, Einstein ran away to his parents house. Einstein’s future looked bleak, however, he was allowed into a college if he passed the entrance exams. Einstein failed 3 categories but his math and physics scores were exceptional. The college agreed to let him in as long as he finished his formal schooling.

Inventing

After college, Einstein saw a low point in his life. He was unable to find a job to support his family and his father’s company had fallen. Einstein tried to make money through tutoring but was fired soon after. Einstein finally got a job through a friend who had recommended him for a position as a clerk. Shortly afterwards, Einstein’s father became ill and passed away. In 1903, Einstein married Maric with his slowly steadying income and began to start researching into science again. In 1905, Einstein published 4 papers, in which he shook the world of modern physics. In the papers, Einstein applied quantum physics to explain the photoelectric effect, calculated the size of juggling atoms in Avogrado’s law, established his theory of special relativity, and provided the first mechanism to explain the sun and stars energy source. *1905 was considered Einstein’s ‘golden year’*
