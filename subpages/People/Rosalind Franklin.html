Rosalind Franklin

About

Rosalind Elsie Franklin was born July 25, 1920, in Notting Hill, London, England into a rich and influential family. She is best known for her contributions in the field of chemistry, more specifically involving DNA. Still early in Rosalind Franklin’s life, she was diagnosed with Ovarian Cancer. She continued to work on her research up to her death on April 16, 1958, in London, England at the age of 37 years old. Franklin is remembered to the world for her intelligence in chemistry and her findings with DNA.

Education

Franklin was a student at St. Paul’s Girls’ School before she attended Newnham College, University of Cambridge, where she studied Physical Chemistry. After graduation, she used her new knowledge in Physical Chemistry to earn a fellowship at Cambridge where she would be able to continue her study in the field. Carbon and coal’s physical chemistry research allowed her to create her doctoral thesis, allowing her to get her doctorate degree from Cambridge University.

Work

However, with the advancement of World War II her fellowship soon took an abrupt halt when she went to work for the British Coal Utilization Research Association. There she researched carbon and coal’s physical chemistry to support the war effort in Britain. After schooling Rosalind Franklin worked with Jacques Méring studying X-ray diffraction from 1947-1950. The methods found would later be used to study DNA. Through extensive research, Franklin was able to determine DNA’s density, structure (Helix), and types. These findings from Franklin would later be the backbone to the findings of other scientists, Watson and Crick, who are almost fully credited for the discovery of DNA’s properties.
