Borderless Behavior Analytics

Summary

Written and compiled by Saryu Nayyar in 2017, Borderless Behavior Analytics documents the shift in network security styles from the 1970s to the 2010s. In the 70s and 80s, the theory was a “strong outside and soft inside”; strong firewalls to protect the soft underbelly of data. However, in recent years, the switch to cloud computing (placing data in many places) has necessitated the analysis of what determines abnormal and normal behavior for someone online in a network. We’ve seen the growth of solutions utilizing user and entity behavior analytics and identity analytics, which helps to close the gaps between unknown access risks. By aiding the understanding of how security concerns and solutions have changed, this book allows readers to look to the future for solutions to new problems that may crop up, even if we haven't experienced the technology that would cause them.
