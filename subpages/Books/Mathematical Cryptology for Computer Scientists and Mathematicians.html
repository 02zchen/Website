Mathematical Cryptology for Computer Scientists and Mathematicians

Summary

Mathematical Cryptology for Computer Scientists and Mathematicians, written by Wayne Patterson, describes research and advances in the field of cryptology from the lens of mathematics and computer security. While a little over 30 years have passed since this book was published, most of topics covered are still in use or relevant to cryptology today. Patterson goes over a lot of the theorems and mathematics used to design encryption methods, as well as vulnerabilities, methods of attack, and more. By studying these approaches and combining them with the modern advancementsin mathematics and computer security, more sophisticated and secure encryption systems can be developed, tested, and implemented.
