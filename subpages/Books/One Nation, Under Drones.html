One Nation, Under Drones

Summary

One Nation, Under Drones: Legality, Morality, and Utility of Unmanned Combat Systems, edited by Capt. John E. Jackson, USN (Ret.), examines the increasing prevalence of drones in the United States. Drones introduce a host of issues not previously seen with other technologies. For example, drones cause legal and moral controversy, which is addressed and explained within the book. It is crucial that the general public become familiar and comfortable with the concept of drones as a part of both daily life and warfare, because the use of drones will likely only increase as time goes on. Not only does the book explain the legal and moral implications of drones, but the utility of them as well. This approach introduces readers to the concept of drones logically, which will likely lead them to accepting the concept in the future. That is why One Nation, Under Drones is important: it explains the importance of drones as a large part of modern society in an accessible way.
