RSA

RSA, named after the designers Ron Rivest, Adi Shamir, and Leonard Adleman, is a public-key cryptosystem that is very widely used. A public-key cryptosystem is a system where there is a public encryption key that is available to everyone, and there is a secret private decryption key. RSA is based on the difficulty that comes from factoring the product of two prime numbers.The RSA algorithm consists of four steps: key generation, key distribution, encryption, and decryption. The first step, key generation, consists of choosing two, large prime numbers and computing the values to be used to find the public and private keys. The second step, key distribution, consists of distributing the public and private keys. The public keys are the product of the two large prime numbers and a coprime integer value that is between 1 and the value of Carmichael’s totient function for the first public key. The private key is the modular multiplicative inverse of the coprime integer modulo the value of Carmichael’s totient function for the product of the two prime numbers. In simpler terms, you will only share two values that were found in key generation, and you will keep one value secret. The third step, encryption, involves converting a message to an integer according to the padding scheme, an agreed-upon reversible protocol. With this integer, the plaintext, one uses the public keys as parameters for modular exponentiation to generate the ciphertext. The fourth step, decryption, involves using the ciphertext as well as the public and private keys to reverse the encryption process based on modular congruency. Once the necessary calculations are done, the ciphertext will be converted to plaintext, and the padding scheme can be reversed to get the original message. RSA is important today because it allows people to securely send messages in a way that is not easily reversed. By depending on the difficulty of factoring the product of two prime numbers as well as the irreversibility of modular exponentiation, one can send critical information with little fear of it being decrypted by unknown malicious parties. RSA encryption and decryption are often too slow to convey large amounts of user data but are instead used to hide codes that can be used for symmetric key encryption.
