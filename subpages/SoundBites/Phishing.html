Phishing

Phishing is one of the most popular methods hackers and bad actors on the internet use to obtain information. Phishing usually involves a fake login portal, website, email, or download file which tricks the user into entering sensitive information. Phishing also often utilizes social engineering to create an attractive lure for a specific user to enter their information, usually by analyzing social media or other public information. Due to the low cost and preparation required to launch a Phishing attack, "phishers" can phish hundreds or even thousands of people at once at low or no cost. Studies show that about 20% of employees with no training will click on a phishing link, but this number drops drastically to .2% after about 5 phishing simulations. Phishers also use specific smtp servers requiring no authentification to "spoof" their email, making it look as if it is from a legitimate source by editing the "From" tab. Generally, however, Google and Microsoft will send spoofed emails to spam or warn of a potential phishing attack. Phishing is one of the easiest to use, most popular, and most successful cyber threats to businesses and individuals, but rising awareness of the issue has been able to mitigate its impact.
