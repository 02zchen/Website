Big Data

Technological per-capita capacity to store information has roughly doubled every 40 monthssince the 1980s; ​by 2020, the amount of digital information available will have grown fromaround 5 zettabytes today to 50 zettabytes (1 zettabyte is 1 sextillion bytes). The rapid growth ofthe amount of data is due to the fact that every machine we interact with leaves a digitalfootprint: computers, GPS, cars, phones, and the list goes on and on.However, data scientists and tech companies alike have run into an issue: the methods that haveworked up until this point to process all incoming data are no longer working. Spreadsheets andtables cannot handle this monster. But, this data is immensely important. It is used to predicttraffic patterns, combat cyber attacks, determine recommendation algorithms, and millions ofother things.In order to process this data, we look to emerging machine learning algorithms and less humaninteraction. It’s important to understand the sheer volume of data and how it basically has arecord of everything you do. In the near future, and even at this moment, companies are beingconfronted on the personal data they may store about you.
