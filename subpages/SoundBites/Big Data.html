Big Data

Technological per-capita capacity to store information has roughly doubled every 40 months since the 1980s; by 2020, the amount of digital information available will have grown from around 5 zettabytes today to 50 zettabytes (1 zettabyte is 1 sextillion bytes). The rapid growth of the amount of data is due to the fact that every machine we interact with leaves a digital footprint: computers, GPS, cars, phones, and the list goes on and on. However, data scientists and tech companies alike have run into an issue: the methods that have worked up until this point to process all incoming data are no longer working. Spreadsheets and tables cannot handle this monster. But, this data is immensely important. It is used to predict traffic patterns, combat cyber-attacks, determine recommendation algorithms, and millions of other things. In order to process this data, we look to emerging machine learning algorithms and less human interaction.
