DMZ

In computer security, it is essential that you keep your internal network separate and secure from unwanted traffic. One of the best ways to accomplish this is to create a demilitarized zone, also referred to as a DMZ, which applies the same principals as the military term. DMZs provide an additional layer of security for your network through the utilization of firewalls. In essence, a firewall is placed in between your internal network, the network your devices connect to, and the internet or an external network. Thus creating a DMZ that acts as a gateway between your network and the public internet. The DMZ functions as a small, isolated network positioned between the Internet and the private network and, if its design is effective, allows the organization extra time to detect and address breaches and threats before they would penetrate the internal networks.
